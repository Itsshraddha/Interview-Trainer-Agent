KNOWLEDGE BASE: General Interview Tips & Preparation Guidelines
================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 1: BEFORE THE INTERVIEW
------------------------------------------------------------------

Research the company:
- Understand the business model, revenue streams, and competitive landscape.
- Read the last 2-3 earnings calls or press releases (for public companies).
- Identify the company's stated values and recent news.
- Look up your interviewers on LinkedIn — understand their background and what they may care about.
- Know the job description line-by-line; map your experience to each key requirement.

Prepare your story bank:
- Write out 6-8 strong STAR stories from your career.
- Map each story to common competencies: leadership, collaboration, problem-solving, failure, initiative.
- Practice telling each story in under 2 minutes.
- Quantify every result (even estimates are better than no numbers).

Logistics preparation:
- Confirm time zone for remote interviews.
- Test your audio/video setup 30 minutes before.
- Have water, pen, and paper nearby.
- Have a copy of your resume and the job description visible.
- Arrive 10-15 minutes early for in-person interviews.

------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 2: DURING THE INTERVIEW
------------------------------------------------------------------

First impressions:
- Greet the interviewer by name.
- Maintain eye contact (look at the camera for video calls, not your own image).
- Match the interviewer's energy level — be professional but warm.
- Have a crisp 60-second professional summary ready for "Tell me about yourself."

Tell me about yourself — structure:
1. Present: current role and main responsibilities (2-3 sentences).
2. Past: 1-2 key experiences or achievements that led here.
3. Future: why you are excited about this specific role and company.

Handling difficult questions:
- If you don't know an answer: say "That's a great question, let me think through it" and pause.
- For estimation questions: think aloud, state your assumptions, show your reasoning process.
- For hypothetical scenarios: ask clarifying questions before jumping to an answer.
- Never lie — if you don't know something, say "I haven't worked with that directly, but here's how I would approach learning it."

Technical interview tips:
- For coding questions: clarify constraints, think aloud, start with brute force and optimize.
- State time and space complexity before and after optimization.
- Test your solution with edge cases (empty input, single element, duplicates, negatives).
- For system design: always start with requirements clarification, then high-level design, then deep-dives.

Body language:
- Lean slightly forward to signal engagement.
- Avoid crossing arms.
- Nod when the interviewer is speaking.
- Smile — it is appropriate even in technical interviews.

------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 3: ASKING QUESTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------------

Always prepare 4-5 thoughtful questions. Great questions to ask:

1. "What does success look like in this role in the first 90 days?"
2. "What are the biggest challenges someone in this role typically faces?"
3. "How does the team handle disagreements on technical direction?"
4. "What does the career growth path look like from this role?"
5. "What do you personally enjoy most about working here?"

Questions to avoid:
- "What does the company do?" (shows lack of research)
- "How much vacation do I get?" (save for the offer stage)
- "Will I be able to work from home?" (save for the offer stage)
- "How quickly can I get promoted?" (too forward for a first interview)

------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 4: AFTER THE INTERVIEW
------------------------------------------------------------------

Send a thank-you email within 24 hours:
- Address each interviewer by name.
- Reference a specific topic from the conversation.
- Reiterate your interest in the role and why you are a strong fit.
- Keep it brief (3-5 sentences).

If you don't hear back:
- Follow up after 5-7 business days with a polite email.
- Do not follow up more than twice.
- Continue your job search — never put all your eggs in one basket.

Handling offers:
- It is always appropriate to ask for 24-48 hours to consider.
- Research market compensation (levels.fyi, Glassdoor, Blind) before negotiating.
- Negotiate from a data-driven position, not emotional appeal.
- Consider the full package: base, bonus, equity, benefits, growth, culture.

------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 5: COMMON INTERVIEW MISTAKES TO AVOID
------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Not researching the company before the interview.
2. Giving vague answers without specific examples ("I'm a great communicator" vs. a STAR story).
3. Speaking negatively about a previous employer or colleague.
4. Failing to ask thoughtful questions.
5. Not quantifying achievements ("I improved performance" vs. "I reduced API latency by 40%").
6. Rambling — practice keeping answers concise and structured.
7. Interrupting the interviewer.
8. Claiming full credit for team achievements — use "I" for your actions and "we" for team outcomes.
9. Not following up with a thank-you message.
10. Accepting or declining an offer in the moment — always give yourself time to think.

------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 6: SALARY NEGOTIATION TIPS
------------------------------------------------------------------

- Research market rates for the role, level, and location before any negotiation.
- Let the employer make the first offer if possible.
- Counter with a specific number, not a range.
- Always negotiate — the worst they can say is no, and most companies expect it.
- Frame negotiation around your value and market data, not personal need.
- Consider the full compensation package: base salary, bonus, equity (RSUs/options), 401k match, health insurance, PTO, remote flexibility, signing bonus.
- If base is fixed, negotiate for signing bonus, additional equity, or earlier performance review.
- Get all final offers in writing before resigning from your current role.

------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 7: EXPERIENCE LEVEL EXPECTATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------------

Entry Level (0-2 years):
- Strong fundamentals are expected (data structures, algorithms, core domain knowledge).
- Demonstrated ability to learn quickly.
- Good communication and collaborative attitude.
- 1-2 internships, projects, or contributions are differentiating factors.

Mid Level (2-5 years):
- Independently own and deliver features/projects.
- Demonstrated ability to navigate ambiguity.
- Beginning to mentor junior team members.
- Strong grasp of system design basics.
- Consistent track record of impact across multiple projects.

Senior Level (5+ years):
- Define technical direction and influence broader architecture.
- Strong system design skills — design at scale.
- Mentorship and knowledge-sharing are expected, not optional.
- Stakeholder management and cross-team influence.
- Demonstrated ability to deliver complex, multi-month initiatives.
